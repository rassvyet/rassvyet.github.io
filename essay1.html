Abstract: This essay examines the psychoanalytic benefits of narrativizing your life to renourish a sense of self, arguing that various exercises - “past life exercises” and such allow a person to interact with unconscious elements of their mind to identify and confront repressions and complexes that would otherwise be ignored. I further this therapeutic model by insisting on a regimen that emphasises living in “silence” (i.e., depriving oneself of social media, excessive music, etc) in order to stabilize the anxious state that our minds are so used to living in, in hopes to show that we can access stable, deep thought by working and existing in periods of lengthy silence throughout our days. By sitting in silence, I’ve observed that I’m able to access deep thinking, and subsequently stimulate my mind and thinking much more than when i am multitasking and spreading my focus over an array of tasks that actually just drain my psyche and breed passivity. In the last leg of the essay, i explain (in a somewhat conspiratorial way) that our capitalist world is effectively trying to produce docile and passive bodies that are psychically too weak to revolt on an individual and societal level; and that social media has become the predominant conditioning medium (or put simply, drug) that this is achieved within.

 "Of the innumerable effects, or impressions, of which the heart, the intellect, or (more generally) the soul is susceptible, what one shall I, on the present occasion, select?” Having chosen a novel, first, and secondly a vivid effect, I consider whether it can be best wrought by incident or tone—whether by ordinary incidents and peculiar tone, or the converse, or by peculiarity both of incident and tone—afterward looking about me (or rather within) for such combinations of event, or tone, as shall best aid me in the construction of the effect." 

 

edgar allen poe - philosophy of composition

 

In this essay, and more specifically in this selected passage, Poe is outlining his creative process in writing. He explains how he chooses his medium first (poem, novel, etc), then the effect (melancholic, empowering, etc) and decides how this is achieved best - wrought upon by a series of incidents of a particular nature, or with a tone, or both. What I find interesting about this process is the reflexive benefit that story-telling can have for a person. Applied individually and inwards, you can see writing as a tool to extrapolate an internal feeling and create a narrative purpose in that. So instead of creating an effect, you start at the effect and work backwards, seeing which incidents and which tones have coloured your experience in the way that it is so coloured. It is very useful, helpful for people to face their repressions via storytelling. the unconscious already tries to whisper truths to us through our dreams, but unfortunately the jaunted morning brain represses it all over again, being taken by complete surprise and against all consent. however, if that same person commits to writing out a story of their choice, they may find that similar to the exercise of "explaining what you think you were in your past life", you will confront the truths you hold about yourself. 

 

Past life exercise: benefits are that you abstract away from your real life limitations and considerations. you can safely admit that in your past life you believe you were a jester, whom everyone laughed at, but you secretly knew the darkest truths about those around you, and by your own unacknowledged grace and kindness, decided to allow everyone to remain happy and undisturbed about their own wickedness. but if i were to ask what i think i am in my current life, the answer would be mundane, so to not sound fantastical, egomaniac, or to dismiss the good relations i do have in my current life (parents, friends,). that is why the question only works in a fantasy setting, where the mind is able to fully express its own feelings without fear that it is negating the things of its real life; the aspects it truly does love and cherish. past life exercises allow you to draw this conclusion about your jestered fantasies - you believe that you are the one true good out of a crowd of bad, that you are fated to be underappreciated, that you hold some mystic power over everyone among you, and that you are ultimately virtuous because you have chosen not to destroy the happiness of others around you (many assumptions here). from this point, i can extract/sift the raw unprocessed feelings: societal exclusion, abnormality, an eternal fate of being ridiculed, low self-image grating against the ego's inflation, etc. if i were to now plug that into telling a story, i may write a beautiful tale (that actually functions as a solution) to my situation:: the jester in my story remains principled, and one day receives the regard and respect of the crowd. They recognize in him the beautiful qualities once overlooked, and our character finally reaches peace with what he knew all along. However, during this story will be scenarios where the jester is heavily ridiculed, and raging moments where he nearly chooses the easier (and uncharacteristic) path, but eventually recommits to the overarching goal. The individual writing this story will naturally find allegorical and personal purpose in their own life, by seeing what their individual incidents are really a part of. 

 

People feel misery when they think the things that happen to them are not for their benefit, but for their fated demise. when they realize that every event can be an opportunity for greatness and a pivot for their own character development, they will find the inner drive to rise above it. Silence as Stimulation The imperative cognitive state for deep thought The research done on our brains point to the fact that multitasking is not conducive to focusing. Our minds need time to process information both analytically (which we can train and do quite easily) and also emotionally (which is done at a slower rate, and through a more unconscious register that needs to operatively digest the information and construct a narrative for what it means in a larger framework.) 

 

Put shortly, we are both thinking about information as well as thinking about what the information means. If we blast ourselves with various tasks, all unrelated to our personal goals, this creates a friction between our analytical mind and our will. You will legitimately not conjure the focus required for the task. Your analytical gears will stall in protest to the unsatisfying work. But of course the point isn’t to always be doing what aligns with your values - many tasks need to be done irrespective of whether it makes your spiritual psyche whole. But the point is to do the task in a pace and rate that lets you think at the highest level you can. Silence As Stimulation is a new phenomenon (and I hope, trend) that will be the first step towards fixing the attentive focus of the non-reading generation.

On Narratizing in bad ways: There is no surprise that while narrativizing your life can yield positive results for the psyche, it can also be a tool for reinstantiating our darkest complexes and fears. The way this is best illustrated is in the example of dream interpretation. Symbols don’t need to be taken literally. Let us suppose that I have a dream where my partner is fooling around with my sister. There are many symbols scattered throughout this dream already, but the first issue is separating the trivial details and the symbols that require analysis. What I mean is that in the dream realm, it is no different really from our waking reality, except that our unconscious operates unrestrictedly more or less, and that what we consider “irrational” in reality can become rational in dream reality. Because of this, there may be an array of nonsensical details that occur in our dreams that may not necessarily point to anything serious. Whether the monster was blue or green only matters if the symbolic difference between those colours are of interest, fascination or relevance to you. But anyways, proceeding from this first notion, it should also be obvious that although something can be symbolic, such as it being my sister that my partner is fooling around with and not some random woman on the street, may not lend itself much importance right away. Sometimes the figures that appear in the dream world are vessels, or stand-ins for the actual fear or thought underneath. But of course, for the dream to be legible and to feel ‘real’ to our psyche, it has to be a suitable, sufficient figure that can exhibit and allow the fear to actually roar with all its might. In other words, the unconscious picks the perfect costume/figure for the fear to wear during the dream. In situations like that, it may not be that i actually fear my partner is on to my sister, but that my true fear is that he would prefer another person, but the way that i could actually become afraid of that visually is if the person was very similar to me in the dream sequence. Dreams are a lot like films, where the unconscious is the director that casts the right characters and figures to bring the script to life. If your script, which represents your complexes and dangerous narratives, is telling you that you are inferior, your dream might cast your sister as the person who your partner cheats on you with. Why would she be casted? Because she is very similar to you in terms of appearance and base personality traits, it will feel “real” in your mind as you watch the scene and dreamena evolve (dream+cinema). The idea of narrating in a way that is conducive to your happiness is a much larger discussion than the one i’ve surveyed here. The core idea of this passage is to emphasize that symbols are not always what they appear, and that there can be various details in your dreams that don’t require heavy analysis. Just like the mis-en-scene of a film, they help bring the script to life aesthetically, but they are not individually rich enough to constitute as symbols. You run the risk of misinterpreting the entire dream if you take a trivial background detail (the colour of the monster’s skin) and assign it serious weight (i.e., “I am afraid of sickly green people”), which would naturally alter the overall message of the dreamena (e.g., if the story going from your fear of creatures under your bed to which type of monster exactly you fear). As well, your dream could have meanings in relation to recent life events. The fight recently had with a partner could be what informs the betrayal had in the cheating dream. It may not be about the character casted as the perpetrator of infidelity (the sister), but that the overarching sense of insecurity in the relationship was the true symbol. If a husband dreams that he murdered his family, it would be worthwhile to examine whether he had been arguing with his family that week or not, because it could move us away from the interpretation of him wanting to leave his family to that he felt isolated, unloved, and wanted to end his pain in the way that humans symbolically know how to : by killing or being killed. Unfortunately, those who are in a bad head space typically get nightmares. And those in a bad head space are more likely to allow their nightmares to sway them into giving more weight to their script/repressions/complexes to begin with. This is where narrativizing the event comes into play: delicately deconstructing the event and removing moral judgements from it. Or, the move may be to remind yourself that the fight you had with your partner should not cause serious doubt in their love, and your dream may be an indicator that you need to spend more time unthorning yourself from the effects of the fight. Narrativizing can also be done wickedly and for your own benefit. We see this in examples of individuals who cannot carry the weight of their own guilt. Usually such persons will spend majority of the time contextualizing their bad behaviour, as a way to justify and almost necessitate their bad behaviour to begin with.
