Fire, Songs & Valour


with my love,
i could have filled ten centuries with fire, songs and valour -
ten whole centuries,
long and winding epochs of poetry and prose,
all dedicated to your name

many hymns for your amber eyes and their mosaic array,
that glisten while you ruminate in somber tones,
streaming tears that befall my lap -
crying arrows shot from Olympus' peak

and that beauty...
i could dance to it forever;
to your ivory skin, auburn hair with woodsy streaks of brown,
a look so moving yet statuesque, you are left to wonder:
who unwound medusa's spell?

tell me, how many more years can i devote to those parting lips of yours?
that speak to conceal the truth,
unmooring my trust in you

lastly, my beloved.... I must confess;
eternity would not suffice to elucidate that beauty in your pain,
that kindness that grew out of the shadows,
that taught you the value of tenderness,
since you were not handled so gently in your youth
