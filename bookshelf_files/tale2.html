The Tail of Quid Ruckinus Chapter 2

The beloved castle that our characters harvested all of their memories in was the only place they’d ever known. According to the chancery’s documents, the history of the castle is as follows: the kings father built the massive estate, after having left his father’s estate in russia. Following a feud with his own brother, our King Dimitri left in the middle of the night, at the ripe age of 16, taking with him his personal guard Muze Clés, and stealing a single crown jewel from his fathers safe. The journey took days, and would not have been possible without the hospitality of several villiage on the way, each who gave Dimitri advice on which grounds he should build his new life upon. Though, these were villiage folk who became literate only though fantasy stories, the enchanting fables they all told about the Nordic lands sparked high curiousity in Dimitri. Each lodging he stayed at made clearer and clearer that these rumours of talking trees and whispering streams in the Nordic lands must have some truth. “It’s not possible for a land to have magical powers, the earth is all one” whispered Muze to Dimitri when the host would turn their back to pour out more tea for the nighttime lodgers.

“But Dimitri, how is it possible that in each home we make the guest of, every person we have spoken to about our move has told us that their experience there was otherworldly? Now I understand that fables we learn as children can shape the things we think we see at night or under our beds, but these people travelled there while they were awake and lucid, both in groups and as adults… do you really think the last five villiages we spoke to were under some mass hysteria?” Asked impressionable yet intentful Dimitri as he stared seriously into his guards eyes.

Muze cocked his head to the side and stared into his own reflection in his tea cup. “You know I am not a believer of these things. It’s never discussed in any of the great works I’ve read. But indeed, it is curious that across a ten days travel, each of these villiages who have no means to communicate or know one another, manage to spin the same evaluations about these purported “mystical lands”.

To this subtle admission of bizarreness, Dimitri’s smile had widened into an expression that looked like youthful frenzy, or as others may put it: hope.

“So that settles it. We are going to journey there and live forever. And it will give us everything we need. I will never need to leave, and I will make sure my future people never hear anything enticing about Russia and risk going there like my grandfathers did.”

And with that, Muze and Dimitri, at the dozen villiages suggestion, trekked for weeks until they reached colours of the sky they had never before seen in paintings or even the most glorified fable stories.

Arriving at the peak of dawn, the sky had billows of faint greens left over from the Aurora borealis of the night before. And in the brightening regions of the sky, where day was approaching to coax dawn out, there were swirls of pinks and other petal coloured clouds seemingly inhaling their hues into each other.

Mesmerized by the sky, impressed beyond expectation, Muze halted his pace entirely and took a brief moment to admit the beauty of the sky into his memory, focusing so intently that it was sure he was laying each brushstroke on his memory’s canvas exactly as the model.

Dimitri had been watching the sky through Muze’s gaze, feeling a burden lift off him by watching his comrade witness a beauty he’d never seen before. Strong gales cut through the grass and raised their cloaks, sending the two voyages back a few steps. Like a raised mast on a windy sea, they drifted back by the wind and lunged forward with their stride. They both knew that this was the place they’d call home.

Naturally, after a decade of hard work, the jewel they brought was able to bring a talented crew of working people who bound the books and sowed the seeds and laid the cobblestones and windowpanes and brushed the leaves and painted the murals that depicted each seasons leave and it’s subsequent growing speed.

With wondrous efforts, the beautiful castle became a world of its own. Tidy trees symmetrically enclosed the castle, plotted in a weaving cluster of dense forestry so to keep all wanderers out. At last, the humble kingdom had an exterior wall that would not even allow its own citizens to manoeuvre away from it with guaranteed success.
